SWMMBot App Demonstration

A chatbot that provides flood related information from a SWMM model

This chatbot is meant to provide information on the flooding situation at your location in Do Lo-Yen Nghia ward in Hanoi, Vietnam.The data used in the simulation was for an 84.8mm total rainfall event falling between 15-16 October 2020. The following can be accessed from the chatbot:

  • whether a location is flooded or not,
  • the maximum flood depth,
  • its flooding duration,
  • the time at which there will be maximum flooding,
  • the maximum flooding rate
  • tips on what to do during a flood

Study Area

Do Lo-Yen Nghia, Ha Dong District, Hanoi, Vietnam

The Drainage System

Do Lo lies to the south of the Yen Nghia ward in the Ha Dong District of Hanoi, the capital city of Vietnam. The average temperature is 23℃, with the lowest being 5℃ and the highest 38℃. The map on the right shows some of the flooded areas for the rains that fell between 15-16 October 2020. Do Lo, Yen Nghia is about 61.5 ha in area and is located next to the Yen Nghia dyke. It is this dyke that protects the area from the Day River which lies to the west of the ward. The Co Ban channel, located to the South and the East of Do Lo, is the main output of the drainage system. The drainage system consists mostly of rectangular open channels and circular pipes below roads. The area is experiencing rapid population growth which in turn puts a strain on the drainage infrastructure.

Dialogflow Conversational Flow Path

How it works:

When you ask the chatbot a question, the computer understands it through Natural Language Processing(NLP) methods and matches it to a particular intent and an action is initiated. If the question requires a dynamic response, a query request/fulfilment code is sent as a webhook or web callback which links with functions that extract data from the SWMM model. The webhook is also connected to a database where the location name that you enter will be stored. After processing the result, a response to your question is then sent back through the webhook to Dialogflow, where it is transformed back and returned to you in natural language that is understandable to you.
